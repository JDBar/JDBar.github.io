Sierpinski Carpet in JavaScript

by Joseph Barron

Slow and Steady

My first attempt. Simply draws all the rectangles, even in unnecessary areas. Accurate until rectangles are below 0.1 pixels, then drawing is skipped.

The quality is quite good, but cannot reach level 10 at a high resolution.

Quick and Dirty

A faster second attempt that can easily reach level 10 at high resolutions. Instead of drawing each rectangle, it draws one at each level, and copies the top-left pixels and pastes them into the correct places.

Less accurate than the slow solution, since subpixel accuracy is impossible.

The Best

My third attempt which is the most accurate, and still really fast. This solution renders one level, then copies the entire pixel area to render the next level.

This will render all squares at an infinitely small accuracy. Unfortunately, in reality, this leads to really horrible artifacts without a smoothing step. By default, this smoothing is enabled.
